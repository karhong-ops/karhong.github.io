Virtual Power Plants (VPPs) Software-based systems that aggregate and optimize decentralized assets (solar, storage, EV chargers) to function as a single, flexible power plant, providing energy and ancillary services (e.g., frequency regulation) to the grid. gridX: What is a virtual power plant (VPP)
VPP Benefits & Performance VPPs enhance grid stability and resilience by providing reliable, dispatchable power during peak demand periods (e.g., reducing peak load by hundreds of megawatts, as demonstrated in programs like California ISO and ISO-NE). IEEFA: The case for virtual power plants
VPP Key Components VPPs rely on ongoing electricity monitoring, smart decision-making algorithms, and two-way communication protocols (like IEC 60870-5-104) to coordinate resources. Next Kraftwerke: VPP explained: What is a Virtual Power Plant?
